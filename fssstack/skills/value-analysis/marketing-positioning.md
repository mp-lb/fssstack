# Marketing Positioning Process

This is a guide to define and refine a product's market positioning with user input at key points. In general you should do the first draft and then ask the user for their feedback and input. They may not want to get involved.

---

## Step 0  Specify Documentation Location

Record all findings and outputs in the specified document or location. Ask for the document path.

---

## Step 1  Identify Alternatives

List what people currently use instead of the product. Examples include:

- other SaaS
- spreadsheets
- hiring someone
- internal tools
- doing nothing
- ChatGPT hacks
- Notion
- email
- existing workflows

Ask the user: "If the product didn't exist, what would people do?" Document these alternatives clearly.

---

## Step 2  Define Unique Differences

Identify actual unique differences of the product versus alternatives. Avoid generic strengths.

Consider:

- unique strengths
- unusual workflows
- specific advantages
- distinctive opinions
- architecture benefits
- audience specialization

Ask the user for input on unique differences. Confirm or refine the list based on whether competitors would struggle to claim these.

---

## Step 3  Translate Differences into Value

For each unique difference, articulate why it matters. Keep asking "So what?" until you reach real value.

Ask the user to review the values derived from unique differences and provide additional input or confirmation.

---

## Step 4  Target the Right Audience

Identify who cares most about the advantages. Avoid broad or generic groups.

Focus on specific situations such as:

- painful workflows
- use cases
- high-friction environments
- constrained teams
- moments of urgency

Use forums, reviews, competitor pages, and social media to discover recurring pain points and unmet needs.

Ask the user to specify the target audience and confirm or refine based on these criteria.

---

## Step 5  Choose Market Context

Decide the category or frame that highlights the strengths and minimizes weaknesses.

Control comparisons to shape perception effectively.

---

## Step 6  Write Core Positioning Statement

Use the format:

> For [specific user/situation],
> who use [alternative],
> [product] is a [category]
> that provides [main value],
> unlike [competitors],
> because it [unique differentiator].

---

## Step 7  Stress Test Positioning

Evaluate if the positioning is:

- specific
- differentiated
- immediately understandable
- naturally implies value
- attracts the right audience
- matches reality

---

## Step 8  Iterate Based on Feedback

Refine positioning using data from:

- sales calls
- signup behavior
- conversion rates
- retention
- audience response
- SEO and content engagement

---

## Summary: 5 Key Questions

1. What are people using instead?
2. What do we uniquely do better?
3. Why does that matter?
4. Who cares most?
5. What framing makes this obvious?

This guide is a direct set of instructions for the AI agent to follow and ask the user for input at specified points.
