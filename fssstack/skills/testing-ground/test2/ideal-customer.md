# Ideal Customer

## Segment

Small B2B SaaS companies (roughly seed to Series B scale) where a single product manager — or a small product team — owns the roadmap and has no dedicated user research or support-ops function.

## Why this segment cares most

- **High ticket volume relative to PM bandwidth:** enough tickets per week that manual reading is no longer feasible, but not enough budget to hire a research team.
- **B2B account economics:** churn of one large account materially hurts revenue, so account-weighted prioritization is load-bearing.
- **Existing Zendesk usage:** the team already runs support in Zendesk, so ingestion is a near-zero adoption cost.
- **Lightweight roadmap process:** the team uses a simple roadmap (a doc, Linear, Jira, Notion). They want better input into that process, not a replacement for it — which is exactly why Productboard feels too heavy.

## Who is not the best fit

- Large enterprises with dedicated research, CX, and product-ops teams; they likely already run a heavier stack.
- B2C apps with millions of low-value tickets; account-weighted prioritization matters less when accounts are individuals.
- Pre-PMF teams with so few tickets that a human can still read them all in a sitting.
