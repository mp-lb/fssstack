# How should TestApp be positioned?

## Positioning statement

> **For product managers at small B2B SaaS teams** who need to turn support volume into roadmap decisions, **TestApp** is a **Zendesk-to-roadmap feedback tool** that **automatically clusters tickets into themes and links them to the accounts they affect** — so PMs can prioritize what to build without reading every ticket or maintaining a tagging spreadsheet.
>
> Unlike **manually reading tickets or tracking them in spreadsheets**, TestApp does the clustering and account-linkage automatically.
>
> Unlike **Productboard and Dovetail**, TestApp is narrowly focused on the Zendesk-ticket-to-theme job, with less setup overhead. *(Comparison is inference; validate with buyer interviews.)*

## Category framing

TestApp is best framed as a **support-ticket feedback synthesizer** rather than:

- A product management platform (Productboard's category — broader scope).
- A research repository (Dovetail's category — qualitative research-led).
- A support tool (Zendesk's category — TestApp sits *on top of* Zendesk, not in place of it).

## Buyer-facing message hierarchy

1. **Headline job:** Turn Zendesk tickets into a prioritized roadmap.
2. **Mechanism:** Automatic clustering + account linkage.
3. **Audience cue:** Small B2B SaaS product teams.
4. **Contrast cue:** Without the manual work of spreadsheets or the overhead of a full PM platform.

## What to avoid claiming (until validated)

- Better clustering accuracy than any named competitor.
- Faster setup time than Productboard or Dovetail (likely true but unverified).
- Integration with non-Zendesk sources.
- ROI claims (e.g. "save X hours per week").
- Any specific prioritization methodology — source material does not specify how prioritization is computed.

## Open questions to validate with the user before public positioning

1. How does TestApp prioritize themes (frequency, ARR weight, recency, manual)?
2. Are there constraints on minimum ticket volume for clustering to work well?
3. Is Zendesk the only supported source today, or are others on the roadmap?
4. What deployment/onboarding effort is required compared to Productboard?

These open questions are **not** blockers for creating this library, but should be resolved before this positioning is published externally.
